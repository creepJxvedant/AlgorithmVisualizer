About Bubble Sort

Bubble Sort is one of the simplest sorting algorithms that works by repeatedly stepping through the list, comparing adjacent elements, and swapping them if they are in the wrong order. This process is repeated until the list is sorted.

The algorithm gets its name because smaller elements "bubble" to the top of the list while larger elements sink to the bottom with each iteration.

Key Features:

  • Comparison-based: Compares each pair of adjacent elements.
  • In-place Sorting: Does not require extra space for sorting.
  • Stable: Maintains the relative order of equal elements.

Advantages:

  • Simple and easy to implement.
  • Useful for small datasets.

Disadvantages:

  • Not suitable for large datasets due to poor performance.
  • Time complexity of O(N2) in the worst and average cases.
